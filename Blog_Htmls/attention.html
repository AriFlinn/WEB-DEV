Attention Please!

So what even is attention? Attention is split between two characterisitcs: intentionality and consciousness. Intentionality can be defined as a sort of mental state of being able to direct action. Consciousness is the awareness of that action being performed. Therefore, attention is the awareness and direction of action (notice how similar this definition is to the idea of WORK in physics).
 
Formal definition is as stated: "the state of consciousness on the process of selecting by his own psyche the information he requires and on the process of choosing an algorithm for response actions, which involves the intensification of sensory and intellectual activities."
 
For the sake of argument we will consider the clinical model of attention to be the most accurate means of describing attention (simply because it is based in empirical research). 5 components of attention are thus described:
  • Focused Attention: ability to respond to specific sensory stimuli
  • Alternating Attention: ability to shift attention between tasks
  • Divided Attention: ability to respond to multiple tasks/stimuli simultaneously
  • Selective Attention: maintainance of attention against distractions
  • Sustained Attention: maintainence of attention continuously
    • In addition, there exists two additional definitions of attention:
  • Mindfulness: clinical intervention to increase attention
  • Vigilant Attention: attention to doing "boring", mentally unstimulating things
    •  
    So that is attention summarized. Now what are distractions then? Distractions can be divided into two primary types: sensory and emotional. Sensory distractions is kind of like opportunity costs in the sense that is price of attentiveness to one thing (when you could have attention to something else). Emotional distractions are distractions which cannot be ignored due to illicting an emotional response (i.e. being called a name, memes, etc.). Emotional distractions are probably the greatest and most common means of being distracted. Perhaps there is a link between emotional regulation, focus, and selective/sustained attention?
     
    Now the interesting thing is to think of attention as a resource of sorts, and thus within the context of a zero-sum game; this is simply because people will want your attention (and obviously we want attention for ourselves). There is great power in simply the ability to ignore and to focus; to say no and just to do what ought to be done. Perhaps mastering this skill, the ability to respectfully decline, to say "Na I'ma do my own thing", that is the best solution of them all.
     
    Truly successful people will not spend their time trying to convince others of their success, nor do they need to brag or educate, rather isn't it so much simpler to just show rather than tell a good story?
     
    Once you think of everything in the sense of projection, it'll all make more sense.
